Festival Seasons

Explore the unique food and traditions from Malaysia and Global

Hari Raya

Hari Raya Aidilfitri is one of the most important festivals celebrated by Muslims in Malaysia. It marks the end of Ramadan, a month of fasting and reflection. Families gather to seek forgiveness, visit relatives, and enjoy traditional foods such as ketupat, rendang, and kuih raya, symbolizing unity and gratitude.

Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year is a major celebration in Malaysia, symbolizing new beginnings and prosperity. Families clean their homes, decorate with red lanterns, and exchange ang pau (red packets). Traditional foods like yee sang, dumplings, and pineapple tarts are shared, representing luck, health, and happiness for the coming year.

Deepavali

Deepavali, also known as the Hindu Festival of Lights, is celebrated by Hindus with prayers, oil lamps, and colorful kolam designs. It signifies the triumph of light over darkness and good over evil. Families prepare traditional sweets and dishes, welcoming friends and neighbors to share the joy of the season.

Christmas

Christmas in Malaysia is celebrated with joy and festivity by Christians and many others. Homes and shopping malls are decorated with lights and Christmas trees. Families exchange gifts, sing carols, and enjoy festive meals, often featuring roasted meats, cakes, and cookies, creating a warm spirit of togetherness.

Oktoberfest

Oktoberfest, originally from Germany, has also found its way into Malaysia as a festive celebration of food and culture. It is marked by lively gatherings, traditional music, and hearty dishes such as sausages, pretzels, and roast meats. The event brings people together to enjoy good company and shared traditions.
